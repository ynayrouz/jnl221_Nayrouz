The World's Biggest Data Breaches & Hacks

The data in this project (which is so cool) is from a collection of news stories about these breaches. If you look at the raw dataset, they've compilated multiple news stories from various sources such as the New York Times, Forbes, The Guardian, Tech Radar, BBC, PC Mag, Tech Crunch and others. From these stories, the journalists extracted the data they needed to create this visualization, such as the year, the number of people affected, and the company.

The dataviz uses the size of circles to represent how many individuals were impacted by each breach, so the bigger the circle, the more people were affected by the breach. This also effectively communicates a main idea in this story that since 2004, breaches have been affecting more individuals per breach.

Additionally, it took me a while to figure out what the colors represent because their legend wasn't noticable to me. They use a yellow circle to represent what they consider an interesting story. This is a great use of color because the contrast is evident, and it stands out more. Although, it made wonder how they determined whether a story was interesting or not, but I realized that the "interesting" stories really semmed to be those that got the most attention.

I also think they used the visual element of space to effectively convey its key message that the number of breaches is increasing. By having the empty space between the circles clearly shrink over time, this visualization makes it evident to a reader that more and more breaches are occuring.

Furthermore, they use elements of interactivity. For instance, a reader can hover over a circle to learn more information about a particular breach. Also, if you click on a circle, it will take you to a news story about that breach, for more in-depth information.

This data story is visceral because it creates a feeling of fear or worry. More data breaches are occuring, and each one is affecting more individuals. The cluster of circles at the top is memorable because it reflects this urgency or concern that this is a serious current issue.
